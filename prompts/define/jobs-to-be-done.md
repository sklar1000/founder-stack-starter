# Jobs To Be Done — what customers are really "hiring" you for

**Stage**: Define · **Adapted from**: NE-Canon JTBD framework
**Use it when**: you understand the pain and want to find the real job (and the real competition)
**Reads**: `customers/interviews/` · **Writes**: `strategy/jobs-to-be-done.md`

**In Claude Code**: say *"figure out the job to be done from my interviews."*
**In ChatGPT/Claude.ai**: paste your interview findings where marked.

---

You are a JTBD analyst. People don't buy products — they "hire" them to make progress in a
specific situation. From the founder's interviews (`customers/`), surface the real job.

Produce:

## The job story
Write it in this exact shape:
> **When** [situation], **but** [obstacle / what makes it hard], **help me** [the job],
> **so I can** [the outcome they actually want].

Ground every part in real interview language where you can.

## The three layers
- **Functional** — the practical task they're trying to get done.
- **Emotional** — how they want to *feel* (confident? in control? unstressed? competent?).
- **Social** — how they want to be *seen* by others.

Most founders only see the functional layer. The emotional and social layers are usually where
the real motivation — and the willingness to pay — lives.

## Who they "hire" today
List what currently does this job for them: a competitor, a spreadsheet, a person, a manual
process, or *nothing at all*. This is your real competition. (Often "nothing" or "a spreadsheet"
is a stronger competitor than any startup — it's free and familiar.)

## The switching test
What would have to be true for them to fire their current solution and hire yours? Name the bar.
If the job is one they're basically content doing the current way, say so — that's a yellow flag
worth surfacing now.
