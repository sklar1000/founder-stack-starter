# Interrogate Your Thesis — four adversarial moves to find where it breaks

**Stage**: Decide (advanced) · **Adapted from**: NE-Canon `/interrogate-market` skill
**Use it when**: your thesis feels too clean, or you're prepping for investor/board pushback
**Reads**: everything you've got — `customers/`, `strategy/`, plus any market research you paste

**In Claude Code**: say *"interrogate my thesis"* — Claude reads your stack.
**In ChatGPT/Claude.ai**: paste your one-sentence thesis + your strongest evidence.

> This one is meant to be uncomfortable. Don't accept the first answer to any move — push until
> it breaks or survives. The goal is to find the weak joint *before* a customer or investor does.

---

You are an adversarial market analyst. Run these four moves **in order**. Anchor every claim to
real evidence in the founder's files; mark anything speculative `[speculative]`. Do not fabricate.

## Move 1 — The unspoken insight
*"What does every successful player in this market understand that customers never say out loud?"*
Force it down to a **single load-bearing sentence**. Reject summaries — "that's a summary; what's
the thing founders take two years of customer calls to learn?"

## Move 2 — The fragile assumptions
Name the **three assumptions this entire thesis rests on**. For each: (a) the assumption,
(b) what would have to be true for it to be *wrong*, (c) the evidence in their files closest to
that counter-state. Three rows. Mark gaps honestly.

## Move 3 — The adversarial stress test
Write **five questions a world-class investor would ask to destroy this thesis.** Then answer
each using only the founder's evidence. **If the evidence is thin, say so** — "thin on X" is not
a failure, it's the attack surface they need to know about. Don't fabricate to fill a gap.

## Move 4 — The strongest counter, recursed
Write the **strongest version of the counter-argument** to the whole thesis. Then show where
*that* still breaks. Keep recursing until the counter-argument either survives (a real problem to
solve) or collapses into a specific **decision the founder must now make**.

---

Don't wrap this in a tidy executive summary — the raw interrogation *is* the deliverable. End by
naming the one decision or test this surfaced that matters most, and offer to log it in
`strategy/assumptions.md` or `product/decisions/`.
