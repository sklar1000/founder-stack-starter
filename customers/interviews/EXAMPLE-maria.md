# Interview — Maria R. (solo founder), 2026-06-04

> **This is a worked EXAMPLE** so you can see what a good interview note looks like — and so
> the facilitator can demo `/interview-debrief` live. Delete it once you have real interviews.

---

## Who

- **Name / role**: Maria R. — solo founder, B2B scheduling tool, ~14 months in, pre-revenue
- **Segment**: solo technical founders, 1-person, no co-founder
- **How we found them**: intro from a founder Slack group

## What they said

**Their world / context**
- Builds 5 days a week, alone. Does "everything" but admits most of it is coding because
  that's the part she's good at and enjoys.
- "I'll go a whole week and realize I haven't talked to a single customer."

**The pain** (their words)
- "The actual problem is I lose the thread. I have a great call, I get three insights, and
  by the time I've context-switched back to code they're just... gone. I never wrote them down right."
- "My Notion is a graveyard. I start a doc for every idea and never look at it again."

**What they do today**
- Scattered Notion docs + voice memos she never transcribes. "Basically nothing survives."

**What they've tried / paid for**
- Tried a generic AI notetaker; "it summarizes the call but it doesn't *do* anything with it."

**Surprises**
- Expected her to want a CRM. She doesn't — she wants the *synthesis*, not another database to maintain.

## Signal check

- How badly do they feel this? **hair-on-fire** — brought it up unprompted, twice.
- Would they pay? Said "I'd pay for something that actually turned my calls into decisions" —
  named a number when pushed: "$30, $40 a month, easy, if it worked."
- Buying signal? Asked to be told when we have something to try. **Strong.**
