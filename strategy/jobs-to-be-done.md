# Jobs To Be Done

People don't buy products — they "hire" them to get a job done. Figure out the job and you
understand the real competition (which is often "nothing" or "a spreadsheet," not another app).

> **New Enterprises method (JTBD):** A job has three layers — **functional** (the practical
> task), **emotional** (how they want to feel), and **social** (how they want to be seen).
> Classic example: people don't buy a quarter-inch drill, they want a quarter-inch hole —
> and really, a shelf on the wall and a partner who's impressed.

---

## The job

Write it as a **job story** — "When I…, but I…, help me…, so I can…":

> **When** [situation], **but** [obstacle / what's hard], **help me** [the job], **so I can**
> [the outcome they actually want].

### Layers

- **Functional** (the task): [what they're literally trying to do]
- **Emotional** (the feeling): [how they want to feel — confident? in control? unstressed?]
- **Social** (the perception): [how they want to be seen by others]

### What they "hire" today to do this job

- [current solution — could be a competitor, a spreadsheet, a person, or doing nothing]

Write it as a job story and name the functional, emotional, and social layers. What are they
hiring today instead?"*
