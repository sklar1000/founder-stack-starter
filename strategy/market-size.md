# Market Size — TAM / SAM / SOM

A sanity check, not a fantasy. The point isn't a giant number for a pitch deck — it's to
confirm your beachhead is *small enough to win* but the whole market is *big enough to matter*.

> **New Enterprises method:** Build it **bottom-up** (number of customers × what they'd pay),
> not top-down ("it's a $50B market and we just need 1%" — the oldest lie in startups).

---

## The three numbers

- **TAM** (Total Addressable Market): everyone who has this problem, anywhere.
  - [# of potential customers] × [annual value each] = **$[ ]**
- **SAM** (Serviceable Addressable Market): the slice you could realistically serve with your model/geography.
  - [# in your reachable market] × [annual value each] = **$[ ]**
- **SOM** (Serviceable Obtainable Market): what you can actually win in the next 1-2 years — basically your beachhead.
  - [# you can realistically win] × [annual value each] = **$[ ]**

## Reality check

- Is the SOM (beachhead) small enough that you can *dominate* it? [yes/no]
- Is the TAM big enough that winning is worth it? [yes/no]
